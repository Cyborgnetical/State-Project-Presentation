How Alabama became a state

Mississippi Territory
1798

Alabama first entered the united states as the Mississippi territory in 1798, as a small sliver of land. In 1804 the U.S. annexed some land, expanding the Mississippi territory northward by a lot. In 1812 again the U.S. annexed more land giving a small bottom bit to the Mississippi territory and access to the ocean.

The Split
1817

In 1817 the Mississippi territory split in half, the west becoming the State of Mississippi and the right into the Alabama territory. The reason the Mississippi was split into two was because white southerners wanted to see 2 slave states. In 1819 under president James Monroe, alabama became an official state with William Wyatt as its first governor

    Industry

    Power Generation

    1. Most of Alabama's power is generated by natural gas. Not cool
    2. A good 1/4th of the state's electricity is nuclear power. Very cool
    3. The state makes more energy than it produces so it often exports to other states via power grid
    More at: https://www.eia.gov/state/print.php

    Industry Manufacturing

    1. A good chunk of Alabama's manufacturing comes from Motor and vehicle parts, chemicals, Aerospace and transportation equipment with Aerospace having the most growth.
    2. Total output in manufacturing is $38.40 billion dollars in 2019 with $15.40 billon being exported in 2020
    3. Altho cotton used to be a huge part of the economy, the 1915 blight forced Alabama to expand and move away from cotton
    More at: https://nam.org/state-manufacturing-data/2021-alabama-manufacturing-facts/
